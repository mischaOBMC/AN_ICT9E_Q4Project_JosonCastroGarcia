Recipe:

Tinola is a comforting Filipino chicken soup flavored with ginger, garlic, and fish sauce, and typically served with leafy greens and green papaya or chayote. It's a soothing and delicious dish often enjoyed during colder months or when you're feeling under the weather. Here are the key ingredients:
Main Ingredients:
 1 whole chicken, cut into serving pieces (or 2-3 lbs chicken parts like thighs, drumsticks, or wings)
1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis)
1 medium onion, quartered
3 cloves garlic, minced
2 tablespoons fresh ginger, sliced thinly or julienned
6 cups water (or chicken broth for extra flavor)
1-2 medium green papayas, peeled, seeded, and sliced into wedges (or chayote if papaya is unavailable)
1 bunch malunggay (moringa) leaves, or spinach or kale (for a substitute)
2 long green chili peppers (optional, for a bit of heat)
Salt and pepper to taste
1 tablespoon vegetable oil (for sautéing)

Procedure:

1. Prepare the Chicken
 -In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat.
-Once the oil is hot, add the onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for about 2-3 minutes until the onion softens and the ginger becomes aromatic.
2. Cook the Chicken
 -Add the chicken pieces into the pot. Cook for about 5-7 minutes, turning occasionally to lightly brown the chicken and develop more flavor.
-Add the fish sauce, and continue to sauté for another 1-2 minutes.
3. Add Water and Simmer
 -Pour in 6 cups of water (or chicken broth). Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a gentle simmer.
-Let the chicken cook for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken becomes tender and fully cooked. Skim off any impurities (foam) that rise to the surface for a clearer broth.
4. Add Papaya or Chayote
 -Once the chicken is tender, add the papaya wedges (or chayote). Let it cook for another 10-15 minutes, or until the papaya is tender and has absorbed the flavor of the broth.
5. Season the Broth
 -Taste the soup and add salt and pepper to your preference. You can also add more fish sauce if you prefer a stronger, more savory flavor.
6. Add the Greens
 -Add the malunggay leaves (moringa) or substitute with spinach or kale. Add the green chili peppers (if using), and simmer for another 2-3 minutes just to wilt the greens.
7. Serve
 -Once everything is cooked and the flavors have melded together, remove the pot from the heat.
-Serve your Tinola hot with steamed rice on the side. You can squeeze some calamansi (or lemon) over the soup for added tang, if desired.
Tips:

 Meat Choices: Traditionally, chicken is used, but you can also make Tinola with pork or even fish, depending on what you prefer or have available.
Vegetable Variations: If green papaya is unavailable, chayote or even potatoes can be used as a substitute. The papaya gives a mild sweetness and soft texture to the soup.
Spice Level: Adjust the amount of green chili peppers depending on how spicy you want your Tinola to be. You can leave them out entirely for a milder dish.
Malunggay (Moringa): If you can’t find malunggay leaves, spinach, or kale work as great substitutes, though they won’t provide the same earthy flavor.

Enjoy your warm, soothing bowl of Tinola, perfect for family meals or whenever you need a comforting dish to make you feel good!
