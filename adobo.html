Recipe:

Adobo is a classic Filipino dish known for its rich, savory, and tangy flavor. Here's a basic list of ingredients for making Adobo:
For Pork Adobo (or Chicken Adobo):
2 lbs (900g) pork belly or chicken (thighs or drumsticks), cut into pieces
1 medium onion, peeled and sliced thinly
6 cloves garlic, minced or smashed
1 bay leaf (dahon ng laurel)
1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
1/4 cup soy sauce
1/4 cup vinegar (white vinegar or cane vinegar is preferred)
1/4 cup water (adjust to your desired sauce consistency)
1 tablespoon cooking oil (for frying)
1 tablespoon brown sugar (optional, for a touch of sweetness)
Salt to taste
Ground black pepper to taste
Optional: 1 potato (peeled and sliced, for added texture)
For Variations:
Coconut milk (for Adobo sa Gata): 1/2 cup coconut milk, if you're making a coconut milk version of Adobo.
Potatoes or hard-boiled eggs: Some variations include potatoes or eggs as additional ingredients.
Feel free to adjust the ingredients based on your taste preferences, whether you like it more vinegary, salty, or sweet. Adobo is very
versatile and can be adapted to different types of meat or flavor profiles!

Procedure:

Making Adobo is easy and straightforward, with a combination of simmering and marinating to achieve the perfect balance of savory, tangy,
and slightly sweet flavors. Hereâ€™s how to make this classic Filipino dish:
1.Marinate the Meat
 - In a large bowl, combine the pork or chicken pieces, soy sauce, garlic, onion, bay leaf, and whole black peppercorns.
- Mix everything well and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour (you can also marinate it overnight in the fridge for deeper flavor).
2.Sear the Meat (Optional but Recommended)
 - In a large pan or pot, heat about 1 tablespoon of oil over medium heat.
- Add the marinated meat pieces, browning them on all sides for about 5-7 minutes. This step adds extra flavor to the dish.
- Once browned, remove the meat from the pan and set it aside.
3.Simmer the Adobo
- In the same pan (with the rendered fat and remaining marinade), pour in the vinegar. Let it cook for about 2-3 minutes without stirring to
allow the vinegar to cook off a bit and lose its raw flavor.
- Add the browned meat back into the pan.
- Pour in water and stir everything to combine. If you want a thicker sauce, you can reduce the amount of water.
- Add brown sugar (optional) and stir to balance the tanginess of the vinegar.
4.Simmer and Cook
- Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then lower the heat to a simmer.
- Cover the pan and cook for about 30-45 minutes, or until the meat is tender and fully cooked. Stir occasionally and check the seasoning.
- Taste and adjust with more soy sauce, vinegar, or salt as needed to suit your preference.
- If you're using potatoes, you can add them in halfway through the cooking process to soften and absorb the flavors.
5.Reduce the Sauce (Optional)
 - If you prefer a thicker sauce, uncover the pan towards the end of cooking and let it simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes, until the sauce has
reduced to your desired consistency.
6.Final Touches
- Once the meat is tender and the sauce is flavorful, remove from heat.
- Garnish with freshly ground black pepper if desired.
7.Serve and Enjoy
- Serve your Adobo hot with a side of steamed white rice. The rich sauce pairs perfectly with the rice, allowing you to savor every bite.

Tip: Adobo is often even more flavorful the next day, as the ingredients continue to meld together. It can be stored in the refrigerator and reheated for future meals!
