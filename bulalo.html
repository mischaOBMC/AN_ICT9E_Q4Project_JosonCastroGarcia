Recipe:

Bulalo is a hearty and flavorful Filipino soup made with beef shank, bone marrow, and vegetables. It's perfect for colder days and is often enjoyed as a comforting family meal. Here are the key ingredients:

Main Ingredients:

2 lbs (900g) beef shank with bone marrow, cut into pieces
1 medium onion, peeled and quartered
4 cloves garlic, smashed
2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
1 tablespoon salt, or to taste
1 teaspoon peppercorns
6 cups water (or beef broth for extra flavor)
1 medium corn on the cob, cut into 2-inch pieces (optional, for added sweetness)

Vegetables:
1 large potato, peeled and sliced into rounds or chunks
1 medium carrot, sliced into rounds or chunks
1 bunch of bok choy (or pechay), ends trimmed
1 bunch of string beans (sitaw), trimmed
1 small cabbage, quartered (optional)
1 ear of corn, optional but adds sweetness and texture

Optional: Calamansi or lemon (for garnish and to add a bit of tang when serving)

Procedure:

1. Prepare the Beef
 -In a large pot, combine the beef shank with bone marrow, onion, garlic, and water (about 6 cups).
-If you have beef broth, you can substitute half or all of the water with it for richer flavor.
-Bring everything to a boil over high heat.
-Once boiling, skim off any scum that rises to the surface to keep the broth clear.
2. Simmer the Beef
 -Lower the heat to medium-low and let the beef simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the meat becomes tender and the marrow is soft and slightly melted.
-This will create a rich and flavorful broth.
-Keep an eye on the water level and add more if needed.
3. Season the Broth
 -Add fish sauce, salt, and peppercorns to the pot for seasoning.
-Taste the broth and adjust the seasoning to your preference.
If youâ€™re adding corn, drop the pieces into the pot now and let it simmer with the beef for about 30-45 minutes, until the corn is tender. 4. Add the Vegetables
 -Once the beef and corn are tender, add the potatoes, carrots, and string beans (sitaw).
-Continue simmering for another 15-20 minutes, until the vegetables are cooked through.
-Add the bok choy (pechay) and the cabbage (if using) in the last few minutes of cooking, just enough to wilt them but retain their bright color and freshness.
5. Final Adjustments
 -Taste the soup one last time and adjust the seasoning if necessary, adding more fish sauce, salt, or pepper as needed.
6. Serve
 -Ladle the soup into bowls, making sure to include a generous portion of beef, bone marrow, vegetables, and broth.
-Serve with steamed rice and calamansi or lemon wedges on the side for added tang, if desired.

Tips:

 -Bone marrow: The marrow is the star of this dish. It becomes soft and rich after simmering for hours, adding deep flavor to the broth.
You can scoop it out and spread it on rice, if you like.
-Skimming the broth: It's important to remove any impurities (scum) that rise to the surface while simmering the beef to keep the broth clean and clear.
-Broth richness: For a richer, more flavorful broth, you can simmer the beef
for longer (up to 3 hours), ensuring that the marrow and collagen break down fully. Bulalo is a comforting and filling meal, perfect for sharing with
