Recipe:

Sisig is a famous Filipino dish known for its bold flavors and crispy texture. It’s made with finely chopped pork (usually face, ears, and liver), seasoned with calamansi, soy sauce, chili, and served sizzling hot. Here's how you can make this savory, tangy, and spicy dish.

Main Ingredients:

 1 lb (450g) pork belly, boiled and chopped into small pieces
1/2 lb (225g) pork ears, boiled and chopped (optional but traditional)
1/4 lb (115g) pork liver, boiled and chopped
1 medium onion, finely chopped
2-3 long green chili peppers, finely chopped (adjust based on desired spice level)
1-2 red chili peppers, chopped (optional, for more heat)
3 tablespoons soy sauce
2 tablespoons calamansi juice (or lemon/lime juice)
1 tablespoon vinegar
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
1/4 teaspoon sugar (optional, for balancing flavors)
1-2 tablespoons mayo (optional, for creaminess)
1 tablespoon butter (for sautéing)
1 egg (optional, for topping)
Chicharrón (crispy pork skin) or crispy garlic (optional, for extra crunch)

Procedure:

1. Boil the Pork
 -Start by boiling the pork belly, pork ears, and pork liver in water with a pinch of salt for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the meat becomes tender.
-Drain and allow the pork to cool. Once cooled, chop the pork into small, bite-sized pieces. For a more traditional texture, chop the pork into tiny pieces using a cleaver or knife.
2. Sauté the Pork
 -Heat butter in a large skillet or pan over medium heat. Once the butter is melted, add the chopped pork belly, pork ears, and pork liver. Sauté for about 5-7 minutes until the pork becomes crispy and slightly browned. The texture should be a mix of tender and crispy.
-If you like extra crunch, you can also add some chicharrón (crispy pork skin) or crispy garlic at this stage for more texture.
3. Add Seasonings
 -Add the finely chopped onions and green chili peppers (and red chili peppers, if using) to the pan. Continue to sauté for another 3-4 minutes until the onions become soft and aromatic.
-Stir in the soy sauce, calamansi juice, and vinegar. Allow the mixture to cook for an additional 3-5 minutes, letting the flavors meld together.
-Season with salt, pepper, and sugar (if using). Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preference.
4. Add Mayo and Stir
 -If you like a creamier texture, stir in mayo (optional). This step is common for those who want a richer sisig. Stir well until everything is evenly mixed.
5. Prepare the Sizzling Plate
 -Heat a sizzling plate (or a cast-iron skillet) over high heat. If you don’t have a sizzling plate, you can use a regular skillet or frying pan. You can drizzle a bit of oil on the plate to make it extra hot and sizzling when you serve the sisig.
6. Serve the Sisig
 -Once the plate is heated, transfer the cooked pork mixture onto the sizzling plate. Crack an egg on top (optional), allowing it to cook in the residual heat. If you like it well-cooked, you can mix the egg into the sisig for a creamy finish.
-Serve the sisig immediately while it’s still sizzling. Pair with steamed rice and extra calamansi on the side for added freshness.

Tips:

 Texture: For more texture, make sure to let the pork bits get crispy in the pan before adding the onions and seasonings. The crispy parts are what make sisig so delicious.
Vegetables: Some versions of sisig include chopped bell peppers or green onions. You can customize your sisig with your favorite vegetables.
Acidity: If you like it extra tangy, you can add a little more calamansi or even a splash of lime juice.
Heat: Adjust the chili level based on your preference. If you prefer a milder dish, reduce or omit the chili peppers.

Enjoy your sizzling Sisig—a flavorful, savory Filipino dish that's perfect for any gathering or casual meal!
