Recipe:

Calamares is a popular Filipino dish made from battered and deep-fried squid rings, often served as an appetizer or snack. It's crispy on the outside and tender on the inside, making it a favorite in Filipino cuisine. Here are the ingredients needed:

Main Ingredients:
 1 lb fresh squid (about 4-6 medium squid), cleaned and cut into rings
1 cup all-purpose flour
1/2 cup cornstarch
1 teaspoon baking powder
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
1/4 teaspoon paprika (optional, for a bit of flavor and color)
1 egg, beaten
1/2 cup cold water (adjust as needed to achieve a thick batter)
Vegetable oil for frying
For Dipping Sauce (optional):
 1/4 cup mayonnaise
1 tablespoon ketchup
1 teaspoon calamansi or lemon juice
1/2 teaspoon sugar
A pinch of salt and pepper to taste

Procedure:

1. Prepare the Squid
 -Clean the squid by removing the head, tentacles, and innards. Peel off the skin, if desired, and rinse thoroughly. Pat the squid dry with paper towels.
-Cut the squid into rings, about 1/4 to 1/2-inch thick. Set aside.
2. Make the Batter
 In a mixing bowl, combine the flour, cornstarch, baking powder, salt, black pepper, and paprika (if using).
-Add the beaten egg and gradually stir in the cold water until the batter reaches a thick, smooth consistency. It should be thick enough to coat the squid rings but not too runny.
3. Coat the Squid
 Dip the squid rings into the batter, making sure they are fully coated.
Shake off any excess batter before frying to ensure a crisp coating.
4. Fry the Calamares
 Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium-high heat. The oil should be around 350°F (175°C) for frying.
Carefully drop the battered squid rings into the hot oil, a few at a time, making sure not to overcrowd the pan.
Fry for about 2-4 minutes, or until the squid is golden brown and crispy. The squid cooks quickly, so don’t leave it in the oil for too long to avoid overcooking.
Remove the fried calamares from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
5. Prepare the Dipping Sauce (optional)
 In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise, ketchup, calamansi (or lemon juice), sugar, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Stir until smooth and well-combined.
6. Serve
 Serve the Calamares hot with the dipping sauce on the side.

Tips:

Squid Size: If you're using larger squid, you can cut them into thicker rings. For smaller squid, you can leave them whole or cut into smaller rings.
Crispiness: For an even crispier texture, you can double coat the squid by dipping them into the batter and then rolling them in extra flour or breadcrumbs before frying.
Alternative Dips: Calamares is also great with vinegar or sweet chili sauce if you prefer a different dipping option.
Enjoy your crispy, flavorful Calamares, a delightful Filipino dish that pairs perfectly with cold drinks and good company!
